Born in December 1955 in Honolulu, Hawaii. Son of Jesse Allgood and Angela Paradiso, father was member of United States Coast Guard and mother was a housewife and painted as a hobby. Family traveled to different states during Rick’s youth and parents finally retired in Florida. Rick graduated from Satellite High School, Satellite Beach Florida in 1973. Attended Florida State University, received a Bachelors of Arts degree in Art and Craft Design. He studied Religion and Philosophy at Catholic University in Washington D.C. While living in Washington D.C. Rick spent as much time as possible studying the masterpieces that hang in the national museums. He left Washington D.C. to travel Europe and study from the great works of art there, spending most of his time in Italy. He later returned to Florida and worked in advertising with the Belk Lindsey Company creating ad layouts. Opening his own art studio in Eau Gallie, Florida he created murals, sculptures, graphic designs and paintings. While living there he studied with artist Fritz Van Eden from Holland, who was one of the most influential artist to Rick’s work along with artist James Flynn from New York. He then moved to Orlando Florida where Rick had his first “One Man Show” at the Osceola Center for the Arts along with group shows at various local galleries and museums. He worked numerous years for the Walt Disney Company creating Special Event designs for weddings and conventions. Rick currently lives and paints in Winter Park, Florida.
